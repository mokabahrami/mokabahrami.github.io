About

Building hardware that
thinks like a brain

I am a researcher at the intersection of computational neuroscience, digital hardware design, and machine learning. My work focuses on spiking neural networks (SNNs) — the third generation of artificial neural networks — and their efficient implementation on FPGA/ASIC platforms.

My M.Sc. thesis at Shahid Beheshti University produced a novel bio-inspired learning rule — Spatial-Pow-STDP — and a family of CORDIC-based hardware algorithms that enable unsupervised SNN training on resource-constrained digital hardware, achieving state-of-the-art classification accuracy across MNIST, EMNIST, and CIFAR10.

I am currently pursuing PhD opportunities, with a broad interest in neuromorphic systems, intelligent hardware, and bio-inspired computing. I bring two years of full-time research experience, peer-reviewed publications in Nature Portfolio journals, and a strong foundation in both theory and hands-on FPGA implementation.

Published · Open Access · 2024
Digital Design of a Spatial-Pow-STDP Learning Block with High Accuracy Utilizing Pow CORDIC for Large-Scale Image Classifier Spatiotemporal SNN
Mohammad Kazem Bahrami & Soheila Nazari  ·  Scientific Reports (Nature Portfolio) · Vol. 14, Article 3388  ·  09 Feb 2024
We introduce Spatial-Pow-STDP, a novel bio-inspired unsupervised learning rule that encodes inter-neuron geometric distance into synaptic weight updates, enabling fast convergence without labelled data. A companion family of CORDIC algorithms (Exp, Ln, Pow) implements the required nonlinear functions using only shift-and-add operations on FPGA — no hardware multipliers needed. The resulting spatiotemporal SNN achieves 97.5% on MNIST, 97.6% on EMNIST Digits, 93.4% on EMNIST Letters, and 93.0% on CIFAR10, setting a new state-of-the-art for unsupervised spiking classifiers with fewer layers and hyperparameters than competing architectures.
